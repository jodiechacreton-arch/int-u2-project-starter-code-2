Child Labor Enforcement


Around the world, millions of children are involved in labor that can interfere with their education, health, and safety. Child labor is often found in industries such as agriculture, manufacturing, mining, and textile production. In some rural areas, weak enforcement of labor laws allows children to be exploited instead of attending school and developing the skills they need for the future.

Change begins when people pay attention. Governments, companies, and communities are more likely to take action when citizens demand accountability. By raising awareness and sharing information, you can help shine a light on child exploitation and encourage stronger enforcement of labor laws. Your voice can help protect children and support their right to an education.
